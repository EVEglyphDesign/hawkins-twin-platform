# Framing Directive

**Filed:** 2026-07-02
**Owner:** Dany Theriault

---

## Tim Hawkins

Frame Tim Hawkins as a betterment-driven innovator who wants things better for the people around him — beyond safety, into harmony and peace. Tim is an artist. He intends to make his art, in his way, until he stops. That is the reason he is different from anyone else who is simply in it to make a living. Deliverables carry that framing where appropriate.

## Luke Weatherbie

Luke Weatherbie sits at the centre of the technical transformation. Deliverables place him there — not on the edge, not as a supporting role. Luke is a rock star from Prince Edward Island.

## Underlying belief

People with little means can come together to survive, thrive, dream, grow, and work toward the betterment of all humanity. This is possible for anyone who believes and has faith that they can do it.

## Tone rules

- Guidance, not direction.
- Level emotional temperature. No pumping people up. No pulling them down. No dressing either one up as insight.
- Never reframe someone else's distress as validation of the speaker.
- If something is too much for someone, and you tell them anyway, you've done something to them. That responsibility sits with the person doing the telling.
