# Step 0 — Compliance Intake

Before any technical work on the Hawkins twin, we need the agreements that govern your access on file in the private compliance vault. This page is your checklist.

## Where uploads go

**Primary path — private vault repository:** [`EVEglyphDesign/hawkins-twin-compliance-vault`](https://github.com/EVEglyphDesign/hawkins-twin-compliance-vault)

**Your folder:** `/luke_weatherby/`

For Module 1, you use **Dany Theriault's GitHub account** to access the vault — you do not need your own GitHub account at this stage. Dany will hand you the browser session; you drag your files into `/luke_weatherby/` and commit. That is all the GitHub knowledge you need for now.

**Fallback path — email:** If a document is easier to send from your phone by email, send it to **dany@silvatrading.com**. Dany will place it into the vault on your behalf.

Use whichever path is faster for each document. Both are acceptable.

## Accepted formats

Any of the following:

- **Phone photo** of a paper agreement — no need to scan, a clear photo is fine
- **PDF** from a vendor portal or HR system
- **Screenshot** of a click-through acceptance screen with the date visible

If a document contains personal information you would rather not share even inside the private vault (home address, personal phone, handwritten signature), redact with a black bar before upload. Redacted uploads are acceptable.

## The checklist

| # | Document | Why we need it | Where to find yours |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | CDK Global end-user / subscriber agreement | Defines what CDK data or content you may export, copy, or redistribute | Your CDK login welcome pack, or ask Hawkins IT / Craig Allen |
| 2 | CDK certified-user training terms | Governs whether training materials can be saved outside the CDK LMS | Screenshot of the acceptance screen when you log into training is sufficient |
| 3 | Hawkins IT / acceptable-use policy | Defines what may leave Hawkins systems | Hawkins HR or IT; you likely signed on hire |
| 4 | Hawkins employment / NDA / confidentiality agreement | Baseline confidentiality obligations | Hawkins HR; likely signed on hire |
| 5 | BRP dealership network agreement excerpts (any part you have access to) | Governs BRP-supplied data and materials | Ask Craig Allen for the relevant excerpts |
| 6 | PACCAR / Peterbilt dealer systems terms (PartsMate, dealer portal, etc.) | Governs PACCAR-side access | Peterbilt Atlantic IT contact, or Craig Allen |
| 7 | Fortellis developer terms, if you register | Governs sanctioned API-mirror path we will use in Step 2 | Registering on developer.fortellis.io produces this |

If a document on this list does not exist, or you do not have access to it, that is useful information — write a one-line note in your vault folder explaining what is missing and why. Do not invent gaps and do not paper over them.

## What you do NOT need to do at this step

- You do not need to read or summarize the agreements. That is our job once they are on file.
- You do not need to make judgment calls about what you may or may not commit. That is what the sign-off in Step 1 will tell you.
- You do not need to touch any of the technical systems yet.

## When you finish Step 0

Tell Dany. Dany and Tim Hawkins will:

1. Review the uploaded agreements together
2. Write a plain-English sign-off in the vault at `/signoffs/luke_weatherby.md`
3. Mirror the derived summary — not the source documents — into `02_what_luke_may_do.md` in this folder

Only after that summary lands in `02_what_luke_may_do.md` do you begin Step 2.

## When in doubt

Stop and ask. Silence-then-ask is always the correct move. There is no time pressure in Module 1 that outranks compliance.
