# Peterbilt Atlantic — Community & Sponsorship Footprint

**Primary source:** [https://www.peterbiltatlantic.com/check-out-our--community](https://www.peterbiltatlantic.com/check-out-our--community)  
**Supporting sources:** LDANB press release, UNB blog, Easter Seals NB 2018 annual report, blog posts

---

## Overview

Peterbilt Atlantic operates a formal giving program called **"Peterbilt Proud"** that structures its community engagement into three tiers: signature organizational sponsorships, an employee matching program ("Peterbilt Atlantic Family Proud"), and sponsorships through its sister company, Torque Motorsports. The community page describes the philosophy as: "we have more than just a passion for trucks — we have a passion for the communities in which we work and live."

## 1. Learning Disabilities Association of New Brunswick (LDANB)

**Program:** Signature sponsorship of the **Barton Reading and Spelling System** expansion to the Moncton region.

**Announced:** February 6, 2019  
**Source:** [LDANB press release](https://www.ldanb-taanb.ca/peterbilt-atlantic-sponsors-learning-disabilities-association-of-new-brunswick-program-expansion/)

**Details:**
- The Barton Tutoring Program was already operating successfully in Fredericton; Peterbilt Atlantic's sponsorship enabled its expansion to Moncton, assisting up to 25–30 students in the area.
- The Barton Reading and Spelling System is an internationally recognized approach based on the Orton-Gillingham method.
- **100% of Peterbilt Atlantic's funding** went directly to program expansion (per LDANB Board President Roger Duval).
- CEO Tim Hawkins' statement (verbatim): *"On behalf of the entire staff of Peterbilt Atlantic, it gives us great pride to be able to provide access to this outstanding program for students in the Moncton area. At Peterbilt Atlantic we believe that education is a cornerstone for success and we are honoured to assist the excellent work of the LDANB in this province. By removing barriers to education in society, we know that the entire community benefits immensely. This support as part of our Peterbilt Proud program focuses directly on giving back to communities we live in. We look forward to assisting the LDANB grow their programming around New Brunswick in years to come."*
- LDANB contact at time of announcement: Ainsley Congdon, Acting Executive Director.
- Craig Allen, Director of Marketing, served as Peterbilt Atlantic's liaison for the partnership.

## 3. UNB Faculty of Management — Student Partnership

**Program:** Experiential learning partnership with UNB's Faculty of Management.

**Source:** [UNB Blog, July 30, 2021](https://blogs.unb.ca/ideas-with-impact/2021/09/peterbilt.php)

**Details:**
- In 2021, Peterbilt Atlantic partnered with UNB Faculty of Management Strategic Management course students to conduct an industry analysis on **EV truck market penetration** in Eastern Canada.
- Students conducted primary and secondary research on electric truck adoption barriers and opportunities in the region.
- Key findings reported by students:
  - High upfront cost was a primary barrier, particularly for owner-operators; focus recommended on fleet operators.
  - The largest fleet in Canada (12,300 trucks) operates from NB — Day & Ross (a McCain Foods subsidiary) — identified as a potential target customer.
  - The lifetime operating cost of a Class 8 EV truck is **less** than diesel when fuel savings and reduced maintenance (no oil changes, air filters, tune-ups) are factored in.
  - Peterbilt Atlantic's dealer network was identified as a competitive advantage over cheaper EV competitors with weaker service infrastructure.
- Craig Allen (BA '98) and CEO Tim Hawkins (BBA '91) — both UNB alumni — sponsored the project.
- Craig Allen's quote: *"It was very evident that the students took their projects seriously and we were very impressed with the quality of their work and recommendations."*
- Photo described: Hugh Watson (student, left) with Craig Allen (right) — student/industry partnership photo.
- Article author: Matthew MacLong (UNB).

## 4. Peterbilt Atlantic Family Proud (Employee Matching Program)

**Program:** Employee community sponsorship matching program.

**Source:** [Community page](https://www.peterbiltatlantic.com/check-out-our--community)

**Details:**
- Every Peterbilt Atlantic employee can apply for up to **$200 annually** to sponsor a family member involved in:
  - A team sport
  - An educational program
  - A charitable initiative in their home community
- With over 150 employees, this program distributes "countless" small sponsorships across Eastern Canada.
- The program is explicitly designed to reach communities across the full five-province footprint, beyond what the company itself might sponsor directly.
- Contact for the program: Craig Allen at callen@peterbiltatlantic.com.

## Summary of Community Posture

Peterbilt Atlantic's community footprint reflects several consistent themes:

1. **Education:** LDANB Barton Reading program; UNB student partnership; Peterbilt Atlantic Academy (technician training).
2. **Sport:** UNB Reds Hockey (including the Peterbilt Pete Kelly Cup tournament); employee Family Proud program covering youth sports.
3. **Regional trucking industry:** Big Iron Show & Shine, Atlantic Truck Show.
4. **Holiday giving:** 12 Days of Peterbilt Cheer across all eight locations.
5. **Disability and inclusion:** Easter Seals NB fundraising through Torque Motorsports.
6. **Local commerce:** BBQ events at multiple locations (Kentville, Saint-Pascal, Deer Lake, Dartmouth) that serve as open community gatherings.
