# A note for the team

*Prepared for Tim Hawkins to share with the Peterbilt Atlantic and Extreme Torque Motorsports teams before any introductions are made. Written in plain language, at a length a busy person will actually read.*

---

## Who this is about

A friend of mine has been working on something for a while — a way of thinking about how businesses like ours can own our own data, tell our own story, and protect what we've built as technology reshapes the industry around us. He knows trucks and dealers. He knows systems. He has spent years travelling and working with operations all over the world.

He has also put together a **public-source repository** on our operation — everything already visible on our website, our socials, in the trade press — organised so that anyone reading it sees Peterbilt Atlantic and Extreme Torque Motorsports the way I already see them: as an example of what a small-market operation can become when the people in it care about doing the work right.

The repository is respectful. Everything in it is cited to real public sources. Nothing is invented. It centres on three of us — me, Craig, and Luke — because those are the roles that connect to what he wants to talk about.

I'm sharing it with you first because I want the people it names to see it before anyone else does, and I want the wider team to know what's coming before it lands.

## What this is not

- **This is not a sale.** No one is asking anyone to buy anything. No one is asking anyone to sign anything.
- **This is not a change to how we operate.** Nothing in what he's proposing conflicts with our PACCAR relationship, our franchise obligations, or how we look after customers today. Any of that would be a hard no from me, and he knows it.
- **This is not a consultant pitch.** He is not asking to be hired. He is showing us how he thinks, so we can decide whether it is useful to us.
- **This is not marketing content about us.** He has been careful not to put words in anyone's mouth. Where the repository reflects character or intent (mine, Craig's, Luke's), he has clearly marked those sections as understood context rather than facts pulled from a press release.

## What to expect if you meet him

He is one of the sharpest people I know on how systems actually work. He is also the first to tell you that he is not a natural at small talk. When he cares about something, he goes straight to the truth of it — which is a strength when you're solving a hard problem and can feel abrupt in a hallway conversation. He knows this about himself. He is not trying to make anyone uncomfortable.

A few things that will help:

- **If he moves fast, it is not impatience.** It is how he thinks. Slowing him down with a specific question works better than trying to redirect him.
- **If something he says lands the wrong way, ask him to say it again.** He would rather rephrase than be misread. He is not trying to prove a point at anyone's expense.
- **Talk to him about the operation.** He respects operators. He would rather hear from a service manager about what actually happens on the shop floor than have a strategy conversation in a boardroom.
- **He is not going to try to convince anyone of anything they are not ready for.** He knows the difference between a technical conversation and a general one, and he adjusts. Where he has any doubt, he defers to me.

## Why I trust him with our name

Because he treated us the way I try to treat customers — he did the homework, he cited his sources, he asked before assuming, and he corrected himself in writing when he got something wrong. That is the character test I care about.

He also, in plain terms, believes what I believe: that a small-market operation with the right people can be an example to the rest of the world. He wrote as much into the repository. If you read it, you will see it in the closing note.

## What I am asking of the team

Nothing right now, other than to know this is coming. If anyone has concerns after reading the repository, bring them to me directly. If anyone spots something inaccurate about themselves or the operation in the material, tell me and it gets corrected the same day.

That is a commitment he made to me in writing, and I am extending it to you.

— Tim
