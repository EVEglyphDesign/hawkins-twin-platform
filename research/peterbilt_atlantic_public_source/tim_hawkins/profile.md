# Tim Hawkins — Public Profile

**Title:** CEO, Peterbilt Atlantic; CEO, Torque Motorsports (Extreme Torque Motorsports)
**Location:** Fredericton / Hanwell, New Brunswick, Canada
**LinkedIn:** [linkedin.com/in/tim-hawkins-13869220](https://www.linkedin.com/in/tim-hawkins-13869220)

## Overview

Tim Hawkins is the founder and Chief Executive Officer of Peterbilt Atlantic, Atlantic Canada's largest independent heavy-duty truck dealership, and the CEO of Extreme Torque Motorsports (trading as Torque Motorsports), a sister powersports company headquartered in New Brunswick. He has led Peterbilt Atlantic since 1993/1994, growing the enterprise from a single Fredericton outlet to nine service centres across five provinces.

According to his official staff biography on [peterbiltatlantic.com](https://www.peterbiltatlantic.com/view-our--xstaff):

> "Tim Hawkins started the Peterbilt dream in 1994 along with his father when he began serving New Brunswick and Prince Edward Island with a single, Fredericton dealership. A seasoned entrepreneur whose family boasts over 50 years in the truck business, he soon expanded to Moncton, and has recently grown to include a total of nine centres in Atlantic Canada."

The same page notes that Tim "holds a BA in economics from the University of Western Ontario and a BBA from the University of New Brunswick."

## Career

### Peterbilt Atlantic (1993/1994 – present)

Tim has held the title of CEO at Peterbilt Atlantic since January 1993 per his [LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/tim-hawkins-13869220). The company's own narrative places the founding moment in 1994, when he and his father opened the first Fredericton dealership serving New Brunswick and Prince Edward Island.

The [Truck News](https://www.trucknews.com/transportation/east-coast-dealer-celebrates-new-digs-with-pete-n-greet/1003120730/) account of the 2014 Moncton opening describes the expansion trajectory: "Hawkins' father started dealing Petes in New Brunswick in 1994. Three years ago, they bought the dealership in Kentville, N.S., and they have Peterbilt rights to Prince Edward Island. Last year, they bought a Quebec dealership." By August 2014 the Hawkins family owned six Pete dealerships in Nova Scotia, Quebec, and New Brunswick, employing 120 people.

Today Peterbilt Atlantic operates **nine centres in five provinces** — New Brunswick (Hanwell / Fredericton headquarters, Moncton), Nova Scotia (Kentville, Sydney, Dartmouth), Prince Edward Island (Charlottetown), Newfoundland and Labrador (Deer Lake), and Quebec (Saint-Pascal, Saint-Louis-du-Ha!Ha!) — and is described as the largest independent heavy-duty truck dealership on the east coast of Canada ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jcraigallen)).

Under Tim's leadership, Peterbilt Atlantic has positioned itself as a destination for service excellence for all makes and models, not only Peterbilt ([peterbiltatlantic.com staff page](https://www.peterbiltatlantic.com/view-our--xstaff)). Tim has also overseen the company's move into electric trucks and co-led a university partnership with UNB students to research EV market penetration in Eastern Canada ([UNB blog, July 2021](https://blogs.unb.ca/ideas-with-impact/2021/09/peterbilt.php)).

### Torque Motorsports / Extreme Torque Motorsports (2010 – present)

Tim has served as CEO of Torque Motorsports since January 2010 ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/tim-hawkins-13869220)). The Better Business Bureau's profile for Extreme Torque Motorsports (Fredericton) lists "Mr. Timothy Hawkins, Director" as the principal contact ([BBB](https://www.bbb.org/ca/nb/fredericton/profile/electric-motor-parts/extreme-torque-motorsports-0087-91902)). The company operates powersports dealerships in Moncton, Fredericton, and Woodstock, NB. Peterbilt Atlantic describes Torque Motorsports as its "sister powersports company" ([community page](https://www.peterbiltatlantic.com/check-out-our--community)).

## Character (as understood by those who know him)

This section is set apart from the public-source record on purpose. It reflects what people who have worked with Tim consistently observe about him — offered here as understood context, not as sourced fact.

Tim is a **betterment** person. It is the through-line under everything the public record shows: the growth from a single Fredericton dealership to nine centres in Atlantic Canada, the 25-year LDANB partnership, the UNB Reds sponsorship, the Family Proud employee-matching programme, the return to Western with a truck for his fraternity. None of these are marketing decisions. They are the surface of a personality.

He is the kind of person who sees a speck on the floor and picks it up — because that is simply his DNA. He wakes up wanting things better for the people around him. Not just safer. **Better.** He operates well past safety, into harmony and peace. He loves people, and it shows in a thousand small operational decisions across nine centres that most people will never trace back to him.

That orientation is why he has invested in Luke Weatherbie for the technical transformation, why Craig Allen has been given room to build a fifteen-year marketing surface with real substance behind it, and why the community programme is not a line item but a signature. The betterment reflex is upstream of all of it.
