# Craig Allen — Community & Alumni Engagement

---

## UNB Alumni Involvement

Craig Allen is a University of New Brunswick alumnus (BA '98, Anthropology & English). In 2021, he co-led a partnership with UNB's Faculty of Management that brought Strategic Management students into a real-world consulting engagement with Peterbilt Atlantic. He supervised the student team as they researched EV truck market penetration in Eastern Canada — conducting consumer surveys, analyzing fleet data, and producing recommendations. The UNB blog post of July 30, 2021 characterizes Craig (along with Tim Hawkins, BBA '91) as being "proud to partner with UNB students to help lead the charge in this new endeavour" ([UNB blog](https://blogs.unb.ca/ideas-with-impact/2021/09/peterbilt.php)).

Craig's quoted statement on the students' work:
> "It was very evident that the students took their projects seriously and we were very impressed with the quality of their work and recommendations."

This partnership reflects Craig's dual role as a UNB graduate and a professional mentor to the next generation of Maritime business graduates.

## Peterbilt Atlantic Community Programme Administration

Craig serves as the named contact for Peterbilt Atlantic's **Peterbilt Proud** community programme, specifically the **Family Proud** component, which provides up to $200 annually to each employee for a family member's participation in a team sport, educational programme, or charitable initiative. With over 150 employees, this creates a distributed community sponsorship network across Atlantic Canada. Any inquiries are directed to Craig at callen@peterbiltatlantic.com ([community page](https://www.peterbiltatlantic.com/check-out-our--community)).

## LDANB Sponsorship Communications

Craig was the media contact representing Peterbilt Atlantic in the February 2019 press release announcing the company's signature sponsorship of the Learning Disabilities Association of New Brunswick's Barton Tutoring Program expansion to Moncton ([LDANB-TAANB](https://www.ldanb-taanb.ca/peterbilt-atlantic-sponsors-learning-disabilities-association-of-new-brunswick-program-expansion/)). His role was that of communications spokesperson for the initiative.
