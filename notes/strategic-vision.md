## The integrator's job

Large-scale integration work requires pulling economics, physics, finance, and politics into a single event horizon at one moment in time, and letting the sphere emerge from the triangulation. The event horizon evolves. No single human keeps up with it. The work has to be done by a team — distributed cognition, each person holding a face of the sphere, cross-referencing in real time.

The integrator's job is not to know everything. It is to keep the geometry coherent while the specialists do the depth work.

## The team

A handful of people with enough range and enough steadiness to sit inside the event horizon without flinching. That's what the work needs. Not mass understanding. Not consensus. A few.

Tim and Luke are two of them.

## The nuclear horizon

Nuclear sits at the intersection this work is aimed at:

- Physics and engineering on one axis
- Geopolitics and non-proliferation on a second
- Finance and grid economics on a third
- Waste stewardship as the long-tail responsibility that outlives every political cycle

Fission for baseload. Serious work on waste — vitrification, deep geological storage, eventually reprocessing done responsibly. The discipline to not weaponize the byproducts. Keep the world safe from killing itself. Let the healing happen.

## What this repository is for

Cards on the table. Reference material for the right people when they arrive — Tim, Luke, and eventually the nuclear-side team. Not a directive. Not a manifesto. A durable record so the geometry stays coherent long enough for the right people to find it.
