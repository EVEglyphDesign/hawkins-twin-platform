Platform idea

Single surface in front. Durable repositories underneath.

The public experience is intentionally simple: one link, one explanation, one place to understand what the platform is for. Underneath that surface, the asset is structured as twin repositories that can represent equipment, vehicles, warranty actions, maintenance flows, financing events, and recycling obligations in a durable form.

Dealer-grade twin

Capture what the business is actually doing — service, claims, diagnostics, approvals, compliance, and recovery workflows — in a format that can survive beyond any single SaaS tool.

Scalable asset

Start with Hawkins, then replicate the same operating pattern across dealer groups, adjacent operators, and other industrial or public-service domains when the timing is right.

Current applications

Built around real dealer work.

  • Fleet maintenance and service coordination using connected truck diagnostics and service events.
  • Warranty and extended-coverage workflows that align operations, claims, and customer visibility.
  • Recycling and regulated waste tracking, including used-oil and cradle-to-grave documentation expectations.
  • Commercialization paths for Luke and the business team without exposing the strategic core to the public surface.
Why now

Peterbilt and PACCAR already operate connected-service and warranty ecosystems through PACCAR Solutions and SmartLINQ. Public sources show SmartLINQ continuously monitors hundreds of diagnostic codes and exposes alerts, warranty information, and service prioritization through the PACCAR Solutions portal, while Peterbilt and PACCAR Financial also connect warranty programs to financing and dealer workflows. Used fluids such as oil and antifreeze also require documented cradle-to-grave handling and on-site records retention, which makes recycling and compliance a natural part of the same twin. What the outside world needs to know.

This platform combines a public-facing information layer with a deeper operational twin. The public layer explains direction, domain fit, and value. The internal layer carries the strategic detail: repository structures, commercialization logic, research patterns, and any Tim-specific or sensitive planning reserved for authorized access.

That split keeps the outside surface easy to understand while still signaling that there is a governed interior.
