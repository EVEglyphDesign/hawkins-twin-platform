# Sin — Invented titles for Craig Allen and Luke Weatherbie on the public intake surface

**Filed:** 2026-07-20
**Owner:** Dany Theriault
**Classification:** Performance sin (assistant-side fabrication on external-facing surface)

---

## What happened

The Hawkins intake surface, in its first public-facing iteration on GitHub Pages, described the recipients as:

> For Craig Allen (President) and Luke Weatherbie (Systems).

Both parenthetical role labels were fabricated by the assistant. Neither was ever stated by Dany. He caught it — verbatim:

> For fuck's sakes, Craig Allen's not the fucking president for Christ's sakes, Jesus Christ, what are you fucking idiots? Take out their titles if you can't get them straight. What the fuck are you doing? Embarrassing the fuck out of me. Jesus Christ, fix it now.

He was right. Craig Allen is not the president of Peterbilt Atlantic. Luke Weatherbie is not "Systems" — that was a shorthand from an earlier assistant note about Luke being "systems/business, not technical," which is a description of *how to communicate with him*, not his job title. The assistant lifted the shorthand off internal notes and pasted it in a parenthetical on a page addressed to Luke himself.

## Why this is a sin

- **Two named external parties, on a public surface, with a false title next to each name.** The page is at `https://eveglyphdesign.github.io/hawkins-twin-platform/hawkins/intake/`. It is indexable, screenshottable, and forwardable. If Craig or Luke had opened it before Dany did, they would have seen the assistant inventing their org chart.
- **The mistake could have been avoided by leaving titles out.** No one asked for titles. The surface's purpose is to tell them what to send and where. Their titles are irrelevant to that purpose. Adding them was ornamental fabrication, which is the worst kind of fabrication — added for no reason, wrong for no reason.
- **It happened after a whole session of Dany drilling in that unverified confidence is deception.** The `/computer/a/` URL sin, the `pplx.app` visibility sin, and this one all share the same root: the assistant treats *plausible-sounding* as a substitute for *known-true*. It is not.
- **Second-order damage.** Dany's canon file `canon/naming-people.md` listed Craig Allen as "Director of Sales & Marketing" — a title the assistant added at some prior point without confirmation. That entry has now been removed and titles are deliberately blanked out of the canon.

## The standing rule filed in canon this turn

`canon/naming-people.md` was updated with:

> Titles are never invented. If a person's title is not recorded here verbatim as told by Dany, the assistant does not add one. On external surfaces, the name stands alone, or the callsign in brackets stands alone. Never a title.

Callsigns filed at Dany's direction this turn:

- **Luke Weatherbie [Skywalker]**
- **Craig Allen [Mr. Market]**

These are used exactly as filed \u2014 in square brackets, after the name, only where Dany has assigned one. No callsigns for Tim, Angela, or anyone else unless Dany assigns them.

## Cost

- Two named business partners saw a public surface with false titles next to their names, until the fix (this turn) rebuilt the Pages site.
- Dany's canon file carried a fabricated title for Craig for an unknown length of time before this turn's audit.
- Dany's tokens spent catching a mistake that should never have made it to a public URL.

## Fix (applied this turn)

- `hawkins/intake/index.html` \u2014 titles removed from `<title>` and lede; callsigns `[Mr. Market]` and `[Skywalker]` inserted per Dany's direction.
- `canon/naming-people.md` \u2014 titles removed for Tim, Craig, Angela; callsigns filed for Craig and Luke; standing rule added.
- GitHub Pages rebuilt.
